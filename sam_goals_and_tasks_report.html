Task 1: Weather File Variables and Extremes

Core Variables: The simulation engine calculates incident irradiance and cell temperatures using six specific inputs from the weather files: Direct Normal Irradiance (DNI), Diffuse Horizontal Irradiance (DHI), Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI), Dry-bulb temperature, Wind speed, and Albedo (ground reflectance).

GOES Generation & Extremes: The National Solar Radiation Database (NSRDB) generates GOES CONUS files using the Physical Solar Model (PSM) applied to geostationary satellite imagery. Because these files track continuous chronological years, they capture extreme weather events natively.

Task 2: Energy Output Formulas

1. Cell Thermodynamics (Sandia Cell Temperature Model)
The physical heat of the silicon cell is calculated using ambient temperature, effective irradiance, and wind speed.
Module back temp: Tm = G × e(a + b × vwind) + Ta
Cell temp: Tc = Tm + (G / 1000) × ΔT

2. Module DC Output (Simple Efficiency Model)
The DC power accounts for thermal degradation when the cell exceeds 25°C.
DC Power: Pmp = G × ηm × Am × [1 + (γ / 100) × (Tc - 25)]

3. Inverter AC Output & Clipping
DC power is converted to AC power via the inverter efficiency curve (ηinv). If the input forces the AC power over the inverter's maximum rated capacity (Pac,0), the remaining power is clipped.
AC Output: Pac = min(Pdc × ηinv × (1 - Lsys), Pac,0)

4. Final System AC Output
The final grid-delivered power generated by the system applies fixed AC wiring losses (Lac) and general availability losses (Ladjust).
System Power: Pgen = Ninv × Pac × (1 - Lac / 100) × (1 - Ladjust / 100)
