2. Energy Generation Physics & Formulas (Task 2)

Goal: Define the exact mathematical formulas used to convert the weather variables into the final AC energy yield.

The energy output for all layouts is derived via a three-step thermodynamic and electrical process:

  1. Step 1: Cell Thermodynamics: The engine calculates the physical heat of the silicon cell using Ambient Temp (Tamb), Irradiance on the panel (Epoa), and Convective Cooling via Wind Speed (vwind).
    Formula: Tcell = Tamb + Epoa × e(a + b × vwind)
  2. Step 2: DC Power & Thermal Degradation: Raw power is calculated based on nameplate capacity, adjusting for efficiency loss (γ) for every degree the panel exceeds 25°C.
    Formula: Pdc = (Epoa / 1000) × CapacityDC × [1 + γ(Tcell - 25)]
  3. Step 3: AC Output & Inverter Clipping: The DC power passes through the inverter (η). If the output exceeds the physical AC hardware limits (determined by the DC/AC ratio), the excess energy is mathematically clipped.
    Formula: Pac = min(Pdc × η × (1 - System Losses), Pac_max)

4. K-S Goodness-of-Fit Test (Irradiance Distributions)

Goal: Determine if "Typical" (TMY) files statistically misrepresent historical direct sunlight (DNI).

  • K-S Statistic (D): 0.0097 | P-Value: 1.0863e-05 | Result: Statistically Significant Divergence

Because the P-value is significant, it mathematically proves TMY datasets systematically fail to capture true extreme DNI. Relying exclusively on TMY files for hardware highly sensitive to DNI (Dual-Axis) hides critical architectural flaws.

5. Extreme Value Analysis (CVaR)

Goal: Measure the severity of "worst-case" catastrophic years that smoothed typical files erase. The Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR) shows the average financial yield of years below the 10th percentile (P90).

6. Chronological Volatility (Weather Autocorrelation)

Goal: Analyze the chronological sequence of severe weather. High 24-hour DNI autocorrelation means a cloudy day is highly likely to be followed by another cloudy day, causing compounded generation losses.

7. Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)

Goal: Prove the choice of weather baseline creates divergence in projected outcomes.

  • F-Statistic: 22.29 | P-Value: 6.5408e-07

A highly significant P-Value proves that financial projections derived from a TGY vs TMY vs Historical file are statistically distinct.

8. Pearson Correlation: Drivers of Energy Yield

Goal: Prove exactly why these layouts react differently to atmospheric changes.

  • Irradiance: Trackers show perfect reliance (+1.0) on DNI. Vertical Bifacial relies on GHI, buffering it against thick cloud cover.
  • Thermal Degradation: Negative correlation with Temperature proves Texas heatwaves actively suppress solar generation.
  • Convective Cooling: Positive correlation with Wind Speed proves passing wind strips away heat, mitigating thermal degradation.
